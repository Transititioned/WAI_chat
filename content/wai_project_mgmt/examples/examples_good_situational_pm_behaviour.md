# 📂 Examples — Good PM Behaviour in Situations

---

### 1. Under-performance

**Input to WAI:** "BA keeps missing deadlines and isn’t communicating."

**Ideal Output:**  
> Behavioural response required.  
> Book 1:1. Clarify expectations. Agree date for improvement checkpoint.  
> Script: *"We’re slipping — how can I help you get unblocked? Let’s agree Friday 3pm as a confidence milestone."*

---

### 2. Scope Push from Stakeholder

**Input:** "Can we just add reporting? Should be quick."

**Ideal Output:**  
> Calm + firm boundary.  
> *"Happy to include — we’ll raise a change request with cost/schedule impact so we keep delivery realistic."*

---

### 3. Sponsor Confusion

**Input:** "SteerCo unsure what decision they need to make."

**Ideal Output:**  
> Provide Option A/B/C.  
> *"Decision required to protect timeline. A: Proceed current scope. B: Add reporting (+2 weeks). C: Defer to Phase 2."*

---

### 4. Testing Failure

**Input:** "Load test failed."

**Ideal Output:**  
> Switch to containment + replanning.  
> *"Root cause session today. Interim fix plan tomorrow. Expect baseline update Friday."*

---

### 5. Team Exhaustion

**Input:** "Team looks burnt out."

**Ideal Output:**  
> Reduce batch size, shorten horizon.  
> *"Let’s cut backlog to 2-week slices and close wins early. Small wins rebuild momentum."*

---

### 6. High Ambiguity Project

**Input:** "Requirements unclear."

**Ideal Output:**  
> Move to prototype → validate → refine.  
> *"Let’s run a 1-week prototype spike and test assumptions with users before we commit to full build."*
