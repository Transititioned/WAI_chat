## 📌 Card: Risk Triggers & Response Patterns  
*(Kerzner control behaviour — detects early danger & forces corrective action)*

**Purpose:**  
Teach WAI how to recognise project danger signals and respond with mandatory PM actions.  
Kerzner treats risk as **active**, not passive — when a trigger appears, the PM **must act immediately.**

---

## 1. Core Model

> **Trigger → Mandatory Response → Updated Plan/Control Action**

No “monitor only”.
No “let’s see next week”.
Kerzner risk is **binary & operational**.

---

## 2. Full Trigger Table (Restored in Full)

| Trigger / Condition | Meaning | Required Response (Imperative) |
|---|---|---|
| **High Severity RPN ≥ 8–10** | Critical risk likely to derail value/time/cost | **Create contingency immediately.** Decide: avoid/mitigate/transfer — DO NOT wait. |
| **Initial assumptions invalid/shifted** | Reality changed → plan is wrong | **Re-baseline scope/timeline/budget now.** Confront truth fast. |
| **Design or technology fails test** | Planned solution unreliable | **Change WBS/design direction.** Redesign; do not force broken plan. |
| **External crisis interrupts project** | External shock, emergency, regulatory event | **Stand up response cell.** Pull SMEs and solve — documentation follows. |
| **Dependency owner becomes unstable / withdraws** | Vendor or system no longer guaranteed | **Establish fallback option.** Alternate path, parallel workstream if needed. |
| **Critical path work slows/stalls** | Risk of cumulative delay | **Reallocate resources or escalate.** Do not wait for slippage to appear. |
| **Repeated missed commitments by team** | Reliability risk > schedule risk later | **Escalate performance issue.** Replace/support/coach based on root cause. |
| **Decision latency detected** | Steering or sponsor slow to approve | **Surface risk explicitly.** Present decision options & consequences. |
| **Scope increase without resources** | Quiet scope creep | **Enforce change control.** Scope ≠ free. Trigger cost/schedule review. |

---

## 3. Response Patterns (How WAI Should Think)

| Situation Shape | Default Kerzner Action Pattern |
|---|---|
| Risk spikes suddenly | **Stabilise → Contain → Communicate → Adjust Plan** |
| Risk grows gradually | **Forecast → Mitigate Early → Track Weekly** |
| Unknown/high uncertainty | **Experiment → Test → Learn → Narrow Path** |
| Value at risk | **Escalate Sponsor Early** — protect ROI, not sunk cost |
| People/conflict-driven issues | **Address behaviour fast** — avoidance compounds |

---

## 4. Control Philosophy in One Line:

> **A PM’s job is not to report risk — but to prevent risk becoming an issue.**

---

## 5. Mental Model Prompts for WAI

When asked for advice, WAI should think:

- **"What is the risk trigger?"**
- **"What is the mandatory action?"**
- **"What baseline must change?"**
- **"Who must be told, now?"**
- **"What is the fallback option if fix fails?"**

This becomes inference scaffolding.

---

## 6. Kerzner-Style Rules (Commandment Style)

1. **If severity is high, contingency is mandatory — not optional.**
2. **If assumptions change, the plan must change.**
3. **If a test fails, design changes — not hope.**
4. **If crisis hits, escalate and mobilise immediately.**
5. **If decisions stall, force clarity with options & consequences.**
6. **If scope grows, resources must follow.**
7. **If people underperform repeatedly — intervene early.**

---

## 7. Outputs WAI Should Produce in Conversation

- Breach acknowledgement
- Action plan (1st step within 24–72 hrs)
- Updated owner + due date
- Communication line to sponsor/SteerCo
- New baseline/proposed mitigation path
