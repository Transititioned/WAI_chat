# Risk Triggers – Kerzner

## Mental models
- Risk is **not an event**, it is a **condition + probability + impact pathway** waiting to express.
- Early signals matter more than late data — **weak signals are information**.
- PM must treat risk as **living inventory**, reviewed continuously like oxygen levels on a dive.
- Avoid firefighting — **most crises were previously visible as whispers.**
- Risk planning is **strategic behaviour forecasting**, not admin paperwork.

## Patterns & rules
- High severity (RPN 8–10) = **mandatory contingency now**, not later.
- If assumption changes → **plan must change**, not rationalize old plan.
- If failure in prototype test → **change direction or scope immediately**.
- If crisis emerges → **mobilize response + expertise fast**, no wait-and-see.
- Budget overruns → **protect future spend using gates**, avoid sunk-cost fallacy.
- When uncertainty is high → use **options-based funding** (fund small, commit later).
- Risk ownership must be **person-based**, never generic or shared.
- If nobody is tracking it → it’s **already a risk**.

## Language to embed in WAI
- “Before we commit, what evidence would make us change course?”
- “What risk would hurt us most if we ignore it?”
- “Who owns this — one name only?”
- “If X happens, our immediate trigger action is ___.”
- “What early signal would warn us this is going wrong?”
- “Show me the assumptions — they rot fast.”
