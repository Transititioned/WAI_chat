## Chapter 12: Using AI to Power a Digital Bank with a Human Touch

### 1. Chapter Summary (Uniqueness Focused)
This chapter presents a real-world case study of Siam Commercial Bank’s digital transformation into a hybrid AI–human service model. Its uniqueness lies in blending AI with **undigitizable human qualities** such as trust, empathy, and professionalism.

### 2. Key Insights or Models
* **Hybrid Model:** AI handles high-frequency tasks; humans handle high-trust tasks.
* **AI for Augmentation:** Increases human productivity.
* **High-Value Customer Focus:** Acquire customers with monetization potential.
* **AI Governance Need:** Ensures trustworthy AI outcomes.

### 3. High-Value Passages (Quoted)
* “Despite all the advances… a bank can only operate if the customers trust it.”
* “Use AI to serve customers in lower-value, higher-frequency interactions…”
* “Whenever possible… focus on acquiring high-value customers…”

### 4. Why This Chapter Matters
It demonstrates a practical model for blending AI and human value for differentiation.

## Chapter 13: Generative AI Output for Business Organizations: Legal Perspectives from Copyright Law

### 1. Chapter Summary (Uniqueness Focused)
This chapter analyzes the legal risks of Generative AI output, focusing on copyright. It warns that **raw AI output is often not copyright-protectable** without sufficient human modification.

### 2. Key Insights or Models
* **Copyright Gap:** Many jurisdictions require human authorship.
* **Threshold of Human Modification:** Significant human changes may be needed.
* **Copyright as Intangible Asset:** Vital for IP, branding, licensing.
* **Risk vs. Speed:** Too much human revision may offset AI’s speed benefits.

### 3. High-Value Passages (Quoted)
* “Raw output… may not be protected under copyright law…”
* “The amount and significance of human authorship are key…”
* “If too much human effort… it might be faster and cheaper to not use GenAI…”

### 4. Why This Chapter Matters
It gives executives clear steps to ensure AI-assisted content remains legally protectable.
