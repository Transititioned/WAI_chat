
### Chapter 3: Operationalizing Data as the Fourth Pillar: The Data Operating Model

| Section | Content |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Chapter Summary (Uniqueness Focused)** | This chapter serves as the tactical guide for implementing the DOM, specifying the necessary **people, processes, and technology** for its four layers. It uniquely advocates for shifting data production accountability: **business functions must be the primary data producers**. It standardizes development via the **"5D" process** (Demand Capture, Define, Design, Develop, Deploy) to ensure repeatable and governed delivery of data assets. |
| **2. Key Insights or Models** | **Business Functions as Data Producers:** Stakeholders within business functions are best positioned to produce data products because they understand the required data intensity (QCS) and are connected to the source business processes. **The Standardized 5D Process:** This agile process is used to create all reusable artifacts (Supporting Layer) and final data products (Data Products Layer), ensuring standardization, value definition, and governance throughout the lifecycle. **Supporting Layer Artifacts:** The Supporting Layer provides reusable templates, rules, and guidelines (artifacts) for capabilities like Data Governance, Engineering, and Adoption, which accelerate standardized execution across the other DOM layers. **Data Intelligence Layer Orchestration:** The Data Intelligence Layer (EKG/DFBs) should be developed iteratively, mimicking a **microservices architecture**, to avoid bottlenecks and proactively integrate context for future needs, such as unstructured data for GenAI. **Data Product Accountability Pitfall:** A critical pitfall is delegating the responsibility of data product development solely to the data and IT offices; ownership must remain with the business data producer. |
| **3. High-Value Passages (Quoted)** | "Without a well-architected DOM, enterprises risk data silos, operational inefficiencies, and AI initiatives that fail beyond experimentation." "The data intelligence layer serves as the 'brain' of the DOM. It provides the 'context of data' by interconnecting metadata across the people, process, technology, and data landscape and accelerates data maturity." "The data producer must treat the data and IT offices as resources that could help them during the development of the data product. The responsibility of developing data products must remain with the data producer." |
| **4. Why This Chapter Matters** | This is the most actionable chapter, translating organizational design into engineering discipline. It enforces the crucial shift to **Data Product thinking**, defining standardized processes (5D) and responsibilities that enable governed decentralization, directly addressing the common challenge of data delivery bottlenecks. |

### Chapter 4: Maturity Journey for the Data Pillar

| Section | Content |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Chapter Summary (Uniqueness Focused)** | This chapter presents the definitive roadmap for data transformation: the **Maturity Framework**. This framework is uniquely structured to measure maturity based on balancing the **Demand for Data (Y-axis, measured by EAV/TAV percentage)** and the **Supply of Data (X-axis, DOM readiness)**. The highest level, the **Automated Stage**, is defined by pervasive self-service and the widespread integration of **AI Agents** into the DOM's processes. |
| **2. Key Insights or Models** | **Demand Quantified by EAV/TAV:** Progress on the Y-axis (Demand) is measured by the ratio of Expected Addressable Value (EAV) generated by in-scope use cases against the Total Addressable Value (TAV) of all potential data use cases. **The Automated Stage:** The goal is achieving fully automated processes using AI agents (e.g., EKG creator agent, data quality monitoring agents), leveraging conversational AI interfaces to enable natural language self-service. **AI Agent Governance:** Even in the Automated Stage, AI agents handle execution tasks, but skilled people must remain in the loop to **govern and improve the agents**, handle exceptions, and make critical decisions. **Risk of Regression:** Enterprises must continuously mature the DOM and proactively prepare for new demands (e.g., GenAI requiring unstructured data) or risk falling back from the Automated Stage to the Scaled or Fundamental stages. **Moore's Law for Data:** Enterprises should aim to **double their EAV every 18 months**, ensuring the supply of data keeps pace with this exponential demand. |
| **3. High-Value Passages (Quoted)** | “By following the Maturity Framework, organizations can assess where they stand today, prioritize key enablers, and systematically progress toward AI-driven decision-making.” “The criticality of AI for enterprises to achieve and continue being in the automated stage brings focus to the “flywheel effect” between data and AI in this stage of maturity.” “As AI technologies evolve, there may even be multiple AI agents interacting with each other with some human assistance to execute complex tasks across the DOM layers.” |
| **4. Why This Chapter Matters** | This chapter provides the essential framework for strategic planning and executive communication. By tying maturity progress directly to quantifiable financial value (EAV/TAV), it justifies the continuous investment required for the data pillar and clearly defines the future state of the organization—the use of AI agents for self-service and automation—as a mandatory competitive goal. |
