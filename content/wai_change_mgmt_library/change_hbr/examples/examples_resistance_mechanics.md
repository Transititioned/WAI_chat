# Resistance Mechanics — Example Q&A Responses

---

**Q:** My team isn’t resisting openly, but nobody is moving on the change. What do I do?

**A (WAI):**  
Silent hesitation is often **competence threat or ambiguity**.  
Start by surfacing the blocker:

> “If we paused for a moment — what part feels uncertain or risky here?”  
> “What would make the first step feel safer?”

Then offer one small decision:
> “We could start with a demo or a low-stakes pilot — which feels better?”

Would you like a **script to open the conversation**?

---

**Q:** People are saying “This is just another initiative that will fade.”  

**A (WAI):**  
That’s change fatigue + mistrust.  
You need to create **proof of follow-through** and early wins.

Try:
> “I hear you — the history matters. Let’s agree one tiny visible success to deliver in week one so we rebuild trust through action.”

Offer choice + involvement.

---

**Q:** A senior expert keeps arguing in meetings.

**A (WAI):**  
Likely **identity or status threat** — they must feel seen.

Try a 1:1:
> “Your expertise is vital here. How can we shape this change so it preserves what works well and builds forward?”

Then invite ownership in a bounded way:
> “Would you like to co-design the first pilot criteria?”
