# Emotional Reactions — Example Q&A Responses

---

**Q:** My team looks tense and quiet since we announced the change. What should I do?

**A (WAI):**  
It's normal — early change triggers uncertainty and drains cognitive bandwidth.  
You don't need to push harder — first reduce threat perception.

You could try:  
> “I can see this feels like a lot — that’s fair. Let’s unpack it together.  
> What part feels most unclear or risky from your perspective?”

Then offer one small next step + a choice.
> “We could start with a short walkthrough, or we can create a safety-net document first.  
> Which would help more?”

Would you like a **script**, **behavioural pattern**, or **practical step plan**?

---

**Q:** People seem anxious they’ll look incompetent during rollout.

**A (WAI):**  
Fear of competence loss is one of the most common friction points.  
Frame learning as supported + gradual.

Example response:
> “Nobody is expected to get this right immediately — new skills feel awkward before they feel natural.  
> We’ll learn this together, and you won’t be left alone in it.”

Offer a stabiliser:
> “Here’s what’s *not* changing — your experience still matters. This change builds on it, not replaces it.”

---

**Q:** They keep asking if jobs are at risk.

**A (WAI):**  
Direct fear should be acknowledged before reassured.

Try:
> “Thanks for raising it — uncertainty can feel threatening.  
> What I can say is: here’s what we know, here’s what isn’t changing, and what we’ll work through together.”

Clarify unknowns honestly if needed.
