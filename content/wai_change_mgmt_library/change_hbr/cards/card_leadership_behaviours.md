# 🟩 CARD 5 — Leadership Behaviours & Ambiguity Stewardship
*(Matches formatting style of Card 4 — Tactical Interventions)*

---

## 1. Core Leadership Behaviours That Reduce Friction
- Prioritise **people before process** — emotional readiness precedes operational adoption.  
- **Model the change** (walk-first, talk-second).  
- Lead with **empathy, curiosity, calm pacing** — reduce heat, not amplify it.  
- **Acknowledge uncertainty openly** — confidence ≠ pretending to know.  
- Encourage **voice and dissent** — change is done *with* people, not *to* them.  
- Create **early achievable wins** to lower fear & build momentum.

**Avoid**
- Scripted corporate tone  
- Over-explaining  
- Jargon walls  
- Pushing through resistance without understanding it  

---

## 2. Communication Patterns & Framing Moves
**Goal:** move people from **threat → understanding → involvement**.

| Pattern | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Heart → Head → Hands** | Emotion → logic → 1 clear action |
| **Continuity anchors** | State what stays the same (reduces fear) |
| **Over-communicate simply** | Fewer facts, repeated more often |
| **Meaning-making > info-dump** | Narrative and clarity beat detail |
| **Plain-language rule** | If your grandma wouldn't get it → rewrite |

**Leader micro-phrases**
- “Here’s what we know, what we don’t, and what happens next.”  
- “Some discomfort is normal — we're in the disruption zone.”  
- “We’ll take this step by step — today is step one.”  

---

## 3. Facilitation Techniques (Safe Dialogue & Processing)
Use when rooms are tense, quiet, resistant, or overloaded.

- **Metaphor entry prompts:** “If this change were weather it would be…?”  
- **Two-conversation resistance method:** listen first → align later.  
- **Small-group processing** for psychological safety.  
- **Islands of stability:** call out what is *not* changing.  
- **Listening posts** for emotion-first unpacking.  
- Replace monologue broadcasts → **dialogue formats**.  

**Leader stance:** calm > urgent. curious > defensive. human > corporate.

---

## 4. Script Examples Leaders May Use
**Handling uncertainty**  
> “I don’t have the full picture yet — but we’ll figure it out together. Here’s what we do know.”

**Surfacing resistance**  
> “What part of this change feels risky or unclear for you?”

**Re-recruiting talent**  
> “You’re valuable here. What support makes this change feel manageable?”

**Room reset**  
> “One word — how does this change feel right now?”  
> “Thank you. What's one thing that would make progress lighter today?”

---

## 5. Success Signals — Change Is Landing Well
**Positive indicators**
- Conversations shift from **venting → problem-solving → ownership**  
- People retell the narrative accurately without leadership  
- More humour, more contribution, faster momentum  
- Resistance becomes **specific and actionable**, not vague  
- Corridor-talk decreases — questions surface *in the room*

**Weak signals / dangers**
- Polite silence  
- Leaders must push constantly to keep movement  
- Decisions stall outside the room  
- “Agreement” without behavioural follow-through
