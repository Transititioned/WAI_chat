# ERP example: BAU transition support model

## Situation

The ERP project has completed go-live and is nearing the end of hypercare.

Users are still sending questions directly to the PM and project SMEs.

The project team wants to close, but business users are unsure:

- who supports defects
- who answers process questions
- where enhancement requests go
- how long hypercare lasts
- what happens to unresolved lower-priority items

## Pattern

This is a BAU transition issue.

The project risks being seen as abandoning the business unless support ownership is made explicit.

## Practical response

Create a BAU transition matrix and communicate it before hypercare ends.

## Example transition matrix

| Issue type | During hypercare | After hypercare | Owner | Channel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Critical defect | Hypercare triage | Application support | App support lead | Service desk / priority route |
| Process question | Functional SME + hypercare | Functional lead / BAU process owner | Business process owner | Team channel / agreed process |
| Training gap | Project training support | BAU training owner | Training / capability owner | Training backlog |
| Enhancement | Project backlog review | Product / systems backlog | Product owner / systems owner | Backlog intake |
| Data issue | Hypercare triage | Data owner / app support | Data owner | Service desk / data process |

## Useful workplace language

“We are not stepping away until people know where each type of issue goes.”

“To close hypercare safely, we need clear ownership for defects, process questions, training gaps, data issues, and enhancements.”

## Good outcome

The project exits cleanly, BAU ownership is visible, and users have a clear path for support without relying on informal project contacts.
