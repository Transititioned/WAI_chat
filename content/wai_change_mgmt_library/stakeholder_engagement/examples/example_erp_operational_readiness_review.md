# ERP example: operational readiness review

## Situation

The ERP upgrade is technically progressing. Core testing is underway and the project is approaching go-live planning.

The technical team is confident, but business confidence is uneven.

Signals include:

- SOPs are still being refined
- some users are unclear on day-one support
- training has been discussed but not scheduled for all groups
- business SMEs are asking what happens if transactions fail
- managers want to know whether old workarounds will remain available

## Pattern

This is an operational readiness issue.

Technical progress does not automatically mean the business is ready to operate.

## Practical response

Run an operational readiness review using people, process, technology, support, and confidence as the lenses.

## Readiness review table

| Area | Question | Evidence needed | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| People | Do users know what changes for them? | Training plan, attendance, confidence check | Amber |
| Process | Are SOPs/job aids ready for critical flows? | Approved SOPs, exception guides | Amber |
| Technology | Has required testing passed? | SIT/UAT results, defect position | Amber |
| Support | Is hypercare clear? | Support roster, escalation process | Red |
| Confidence | Are business owners ready to switch? | Area readiness sign-off | Amber |

## Useful workplace language

“We are not just asking whether the system works. We are asking whether each area can operate safely on day one.”

“Where evidence is amber or red, let’s turn that into a named action rather than a general concern.”

## Good outcome

The project identifies that support paths and exception guides are weaker than technical readiness. The team creates a hypercare roster, confirms escalation paths, and produces short job aids for priority processes before go-live.
