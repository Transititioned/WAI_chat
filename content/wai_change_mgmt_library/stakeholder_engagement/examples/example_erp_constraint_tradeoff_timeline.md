# ERP constraint trade-off timeline

## Scenario

A senior sponsor wants an ERP upgrade delivered earlier than the current project team believes is credible. The accelerated date may be possible, but only if several constraints are resolved quickly.

The project manager should not simply accept or reject the date. The better move is to present the trade-off as a governance decision.

## Timeline options

| Option | Description | Assumptions | Required support | Key risk | Decision needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Option A: Current-constraint plan | Delivery based on current resourcing, current environment status, and currently available business input | No additional SME commitment; vendor support remains as currently agreed; environment validation completes at normal pace | None beyond current commitments | Later delivery; longer period of uncertainty; less pressure on business | Endorse realistic baseline |
| Option B: Supported acceleration | Earlier delivery if critical constraints are resolved by fixed dates | Environment validated by agreed date; priority customisations triaged; business SMEs allocated; vendor turnaround confirmed | Sponsor prioritisation; business leader commitment; vendor escalation; clear scope discipline | Higher execution pressure; less contingency; more leadership dependency | Approve support and accept delivery risk |

## Example governance ask

“The project can present an accelerated option, but it is not a planning-only decision. It requires leadership support on four conditions: environment readiness, vendor turnaround, SME availability, and scope discipline. Without those conditions, the responsible baseline is Option A.”

## What the PM should avoid

Do not say:

“We will aim for the earlier date and see how we go.”

That creates a soft promise without the support needed to deliver it.

## Better wording

“We can work to the earlier date if the organisation is willing to make the supporting commitments. If those commitments are not available, the later date is the credible baseline.”

## Evidence to bring

- dependency list
- environment readiness status
- defect / customisation triage status
- SME effort estimate
- testing duration assumptions
- vendor response assumptions
- risks and mitigations
