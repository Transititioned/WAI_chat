# ERP example: post-launch lessons learned

## Situation

The ERP upgrade is live.

The project team is tired and wants to move on. Hypercare has stabilised the highest-priority issues, but the same themes keep appearing:

- users understood the happy path but struggled with exceptions
- some reports were not genuinely needed for day one
- support questions were routed inconsistently
- training attendance was high, but confidence varied by team
- several practical issues were known locally but not captured early enough

## Pattern

This is a post-launch mirror and lessons learned without blame issue.

The project has a chance to turn lived experience into reusable delivery knowledge.

## Practical review structure

Run a 60-minute review with project, business, support, and vendor input.

Use four prompts:

1. What worked and should be reused?
2. What created avoidable pain?
3. What surprised us after go-live?
4. What should change in the next release or project method?

## Example output

| Area | Lesson | Future method change |
|---|---|---|
| UAT | Happy path coverage was good, exception coverage was weaker | Add exception scenario review with functional leads before UAT starts |
| Training | Training attendance did not equal confidence | Add confidence check and floor support plan |
| Support | Users were unclear on issue channels | Confirm support paths before go-live and include in job aids |
| Reporting | Too many nice-to-have reports competed with must-have reports | Use must/should/nice classification earlier |

## Useful workplace language

“This is not about reopening old pain. It is about making the next release easier.”

“If this lesson does not change a checklist, plan, artefact, or decision point, it probably will not be reused.”

## Good outcome

The lessons become updates to future UAT planning, training readiness, hypercare design, and go-live reporting prioritisation.
