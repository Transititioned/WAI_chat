# ERP sponsor pre-brief before PCG

## Scenario

An ERP upgrade project is preparing for a PCG. The team has made progress, but the project plan still depends on several unresolved items:

- the upgraded environment needs final validation
- customisations need triage and confirmation
- system testing cannot start until critical defects are resolved
- business SMEs are needed for process validation and UAT preparation
- the target date may move unless constraints are resolved

The project manager needs to brief the sponsor before the PCG so the message does not land cold in the formal meeting.

## Meeting objective

Align with the sponsor on:

1. what is safe to say in the PCG
2. what is still uncertain
3. what support is needed from leadership
4. whether the PCG ask is a decision, endorsement, escalation, or information item

## Pre-brief structure

| Topic | Suggested framing | Sponsor question |
|---|---|---|
| Current status | “We have a path forward, but the plan still depends on validating key technical and business assumptions.” | “Are you comfortable with that level of openness?” |
| Main constraint | “Testing cannot be treated as ready until the upgraded environment and priority customisations are validated.” | “Do you want this positioned as a schedule risk or a decision point?” |
| Business capacity | “We need named SME time for process validation and test preparation.” | “Which business leaders should we ask to confirm availability?” |
| Timeline | “We can show a current-constraint timeline and a supported accelerated option.” | “Do you want both options in the PCG pack?” |
| Governance ask | “The project needs leadership support to prioritise environment readiness, vendor turnaround, and SME availability.” | “Should the ask be framed as endorsement, escalation, or decision?” |

## Useful language

“I want to walk through the PCG message before it lands in the room.”

“The uncomfortable part is that the date is not just a project-planning question. It depends on environment readiness, vendor turnaround, and SME availability.”

“I’d rather be explicit about the assumptions now than have the project look like it missed a date later.”

## Output from the pre-brief

The sponsor and project manager should leave with:

- agreed wording for the project status
- agreed view of whether the timeline is baseline or optioned
- agreed escalation points
- agreed decision ask for PCG
- agreed list of stakeholders to pre-brief before the meeting
