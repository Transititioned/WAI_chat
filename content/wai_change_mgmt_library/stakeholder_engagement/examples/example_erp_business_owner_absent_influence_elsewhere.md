# ERP example: formal business owner absent, influence sits elsewhere

## Situation

A formal business owner is named for a functional area, but they are rarely available.

They attend governance when required, but:

- workshop attendance is delegated
- decisions are slow
- action items sit open
- users wait for a different person’s view before committing
- the project team keeps hearing “we need to check with Sarah”

Sarah is not the named business owner, but she appears to understand the process, exceptions, workload, and local politics.

## Pattern

This is a shadow power map issue.

Formal authority sits with one person, but practical influence and process confidence sit elsewhere.

## Risk if ignored

If the project only works through the formal business owner:

- decisions may appear approved but not adopted
- process maps may miss practical exceptions
- SMEs may not feel safe confirming details
- UAT may be delayed by late questions
- training may be challenged by people who were not genuinely involved

If the project bypasses the business owner completely:

- the business owner may feel undermined
- Sarah may be put in an awkward position
- accountability may become unclear

## Practical response

Use a two-track engagement approach:

1. Keep the formal business owner involved for decision authority.
2. Engage Sarah as a practical process lead, reviewer, or SME.
3. Make the relationship explicit and respectful.
4. Clarify what Sarah can recommend versus what the business owner must approve.
5. Document decisions without exposing informal politics.

## Useful workplace language

To the business owner:

“It looks like Sarah has a strong handle on the process detail. Would it help if we used her as the practical reviewer, with decisions still coming back through you?”

To Sarah:

“I understand you may not be the formal owner, so I want to be careful with decision rights. Could we use your input to validate the process detail before it goes back for approval?”

## Artefact

Decision and influence split:

| Area | Formal decision owner | Practical reviewer | Decision type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SOP validation | Business owner | Sarah | Formal sign-off after practical review | Sarah validates process detail; owner signs off |
| UAT scenarios | Testing lead / Business owner | Sarah | Review and recommendation | Add practical exception scenarios |
| Training content | Change/training lead | Sarah | Input only | Check language and floor usability |

## Good outcome

The formal business owner stays accountable, Sarah’s influence is used constructively, and the project reduces late rework by validating the process through the person the team actually trusts.
