# ERP example: warehouse supervisor as hidden influencer

## Situation

The ERP project has a formal warehouse business owner and a nominated testing lead.

On paper, the stakeholder map looks complete. But in workshops, people keep referring to a senior warehouse supervisor who is not officially part of the project.

Comments include:

- “You should check that with Steve.”
- “That is not how the floor will do it.”
- “If Steve thinks it works, the team will probably go with it.”
- “Steve knows the scanner process better than anyone.”

## Pattern

This is a hidden influence pattern.

The supervisor may not have formal approval authority, but they have practical credibility. The warehouse team may look to them before accepting the new ERP process, participating properly in UAT, or trusting the training material.

## Risk if ignored

If the supervisor is not engaged:

- UAT may be technically attended but weakly performed
- practical scenarios may be missed
- SOPs may not reflect real work
- warehouse staff may treat the change as unrealistic
- go-live support demand may increase
- informal scepticism may spread

## Practical response

Do not bypass the formal business owner. Instead:

1. Tell the business owner you are hearing that the supervisor has strong practical knowledge.
2. Ask whether they should be included as a process reviewer or UAT advisor.
3. Meet the supervisor for a practical walkthrough.
4. Ask what would make the process workable on the floor.
5. Convert concerns into test scenarios, training points, job aids, or readiness actions.

## Useful workplace language

To the business owner:

“I’m hearing that Steve has a lot of the practical warehouse process knowledge. Would it make sense to involve him as a reviewer so we test this against how the work really happens?”

To the supervisor:

“I’m not asking you to sell the project. I’d value your practical view on where this process could fall over on the floor.”

## Artefact

Informal influencer engagement note:

| Influencer | Why they matter | Concern / insight | Action taken | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Warehouse supervisor | Trusted by team; practical scanner/process knowledge | Process may not work cleanly during peak picking | Add UAT scenario and create floor job aid | PM / Testing lead |

## Good outcome

The supervisor helps identify two practical scanner scenarios missing from the test pack. The test lead adds them to UAT. The change/training lead creates a one-page job aid for the warehouse team.

The formal business owner remains respected, and the project gains practical credibility with users.
