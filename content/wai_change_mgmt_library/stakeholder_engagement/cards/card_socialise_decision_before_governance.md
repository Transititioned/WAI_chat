# Socialise the decision before governance

## Trigger

A decision is scheduled for a formal governance meeting, but the decision has not been tested with the sponsor, key approvers, likely challengers, or impacted leaders.

Typical signs:

- The meeting is expected to resolve disagreement that has not been surfaced privately.
- The decision paper contains assumptions that some leaders may reject.
- A senior stakeholder is likely to challenge the numbers, timeline, scope, or resource ask.
- The project manager is hoping the meeting chair will force a decision.

## What it may mean

The decision may not be ready for formal governance. The work may be technically documented but socially underprepared.

Formal governance should confirm a decision that has been properly briefed, tested, and sharpened. It should not be the first place where major stakeholders discover the implications.

## What to do next

1. Send the draft decision material early enough for review.
2. Pre-brief the sponsor on the decision, trade-offs, and likely objections.
3. Speak with the stakeholders most likely to challenge or be affected.
4. Ask what would make the decision clearer, safer, or more supportable.
5. Update the pack to address legitimate concerns.
6. Make the formal decision request specific: approve, endorse, prioritise, fund, resource, defer, or choose an option.

## What not to do

Do not confuse decision socialisation with manipulation.

The point is not to hide disagreement. The point is to prevent avoidable surprise, sharpen the decision, and give stakeholders a fair chance to understand the ask before they are put on the spot.

## Useful workplace language

“I’d like to socialise the decision before the meeting so we know whether the ask is clear and supportable.”

“This looks ready as a paper, but I want to test whether it is ready as a decision.”

“Before we put this to the group, who needs to see it so we do not create avoidable friction in the room?”

## ERP / technology delivery example

Before asking governance to approve an accelerated ERP upgrade date, socialise the decision with the sponsor, technology lead, vendor lead, testing lead, and business operations lead. The meeting should not be the first place they see the dependency chain or the resource ask.
