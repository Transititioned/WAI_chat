# Coffee chat reconnaissance

## Trigger

The project manager, BA, change lead, or delivery lead is unsure who the real stakeholders are.

The formal stakeholder list exists, but it does not explain:

- who people actually listen to
- who has practical knowledge
- who can quietly block progress
- who can help interpret the organisation
- who needs to be engaged before decisions become real

## What it may mean

The formal organisation chart is incomplete for delivery purposes.

Influence, knowledge, and trust may flow through informal networks rather than official reporting lines. The people who understand how work really gets done may not be the people with the most senior titles.

This does not mean the formal stakeholders are irrelevant. It means the delivery picture is incomplete.

## What to do next

Run a light stakeholder discovery round using short, respectful one-on-one conversations.

Ask practical questions such as:

1. Who understands this process best day to day?
2. Who do people naturally go to when they need a practical answer?
3. Who should I speak to before we lock in the approach?
4. Who is likely to be affected but may not be visible in the current stakeholder list?
5. Who would know where this has gone wrong before?

Keep it informal and low-pressure. The goal is not to create a political map for its own sake. The goal is to avoid missing the people who can make or break delivery.

## What not to do

Do not rely only on the org chart, steering committee list, or sponsor nomination.

Do not make the conversations feel like an investigation. If people think you are mapping factions or gathering gossip, trust will drop quickly.

Do not treat informal comments as facts. Treat them as leads to validate.

## Useful workplace language

“I’m trying to understand how this really works day to day, not just how it looks on paper. Who would you suggest I speak with?”

“I want to make sure we are not missing the people who will have to live with this. Who has the best practical view?”

“Who do people usually go to when they need a sensible answer on this process?”

## Artefact or meeting move

Create a stakeholder discovery log.

| Person mentioned | Why they matter | Mentioned by | Follow-up needed | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
|  | Process knowledge / influence / decision support / risk insight |  | Yes / No |  |

Keep the log factual. Avoid loaded labels such as “blocker” unless there is evidence.

## ERP / technology delivery example

In an ERP upgrade, the official business owner may be a department manager, but the person who knows the process reality may be a senior warehouse supervisor, order entry lead, finance analyst, or admin coordinator.

Before finalising UAT involvement or SOP ownership, run short discovery conversations to identify who the business naturally trusts for practical process answers.
