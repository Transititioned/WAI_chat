# Informal influencer as delivery risk

## Trigger

A respected person outside the formal project structure has strong influence over whether others will participate, trust, test, train, or adopt.

They may be:

- a senior operator
- a long-tenured analyst
- a supervisor
- an admin coordinator
- a scheduler
- a finance process expert
- a practical “go-to” person in the team

## What it may mean

The project may have a local credibility dependency.

If the informal influencer is supportive, they can accelerate adoption. If they are sceptical, ignored, or embarrassed, they can quietly slow engagement, reduce testing quality, or seed doubt.

This is not automatically political. Often, the influencer is simply trusted because they know the work and have protected the team before.

## What to do next

Engage them early and respectfully.

1. Acknowledge their practical knowledge.
2. Ask for their view before the design or plan is locked.
3. Give them a safe way to raise concerns.
4. Convert useful concerns into actions, risks, test cases, or training improvements.
5. Keep their manager informed so the engagement does not look like bypassing.

## What not to do

Do not try to “win them over” with spin.

Do not treat them as a blocker just because they are sceptical.

Do not ask them to champion a change they do not yet believe is workable.

Do not bypass their manager in a way that creates tension.

## Useful workplace language

“I understand people trust your view on how this works in practice. I’d like to test the approach with you before we go too far.”

“You may see risks in this that the project team will miss. Can we walk through the process from your point of view?”

“I’m not asking you to sell this. I’m asking you to help us make sure it is workable.”

## Artefact or meeting move

Create an informal influencer engagement note.

| Influencer | Why they matter | Current stance | Concern / insight | Action taken | Manager alignment needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|  | Trusted by team / process expert / adoption signal | Supportive / cautious / sceptical / unknown |  |  | Yes / No |

## ERP / technology delivery example

A warehouse supervisor is not on the ERP governance group, but the warehouse team will take their lead during UAT. If they think the new process is unrealistic, users may technically attend testing but give low-quality engagement.

The project should involve the supervisor early to validate scenarios, confirm practical pain points, and identify what training or job aids will matter.
