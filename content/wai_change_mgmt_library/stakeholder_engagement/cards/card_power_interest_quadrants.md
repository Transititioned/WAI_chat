# Power-interest quadrants

## Trigger

The stakeholder list is too broad, and the project team is not sure where to focus engagement effort.

Some people care deeply but lack formal authority. Others have high authority but seem disengaged. A few people have both power and interest and need close management.

## What it may mean

The project needs targeted stakeholder engagement, not equal communication to everyone.

Stakeholders usually need different treatment depending on:

- how much influence they have
- how much they care
- how directly the change affects them
- whether they can support, block, inform, or amplify the work

## What to do next

Classify stakeholders into four practical groups.

| Quadrant | Meaning | Engagement approach |
|---|---|---|
| High power / high interest | Power players | Manage closely. Pre-brief, involve in decisions, test key messages. |
| High power / low interest | Danger zone | Keep satisfied. Brief selectively. Watch for late objections. |
| Low power / high interest | Informants | Use for process insight, early warning, practical feedback, and change sensing. |
| Low power / low interest | Sleepers | Monitor lightly. Keep informed enough to avoid surprise. |

Review the map regularly. Stakeholders move between quadrants as the project changes.

## What not to do

Do not over-invest in low-power stakeholders while neglecting high-power low-interest stakeholders.

Do not assume low-interest stakeholders are low-risk. High-power low-interest stakeholders can become dangerous if they wake up late and dislike what they see.

Do not use the quadrant labels in a way that sounds dismissive.

## Useful workplace language

“We need to separate who needs close involvement from who needs targeted updates.”

“This person may not be very engaged now, but they have enough influence that we should not let them be surprised late.”

“These users may not approve the decision, but they will tell us whether the process will actually work.”

## Artefact or meeting move

Create a power-interest map and review it monthly.

| Stakeholder | Power | Interest | Current stance | Engagement action | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|  | High / Medium / Low | High / Medium / Low | Supportive / neutral / concerned / unknown |  |  |

## ERP / technology delivery example

For an ERP upgrade:

- CFO may be high power / medium interest for finance risk and reporting.
- Warehouse supervisor may be low formal power / high interest with strong practical influence.
- Senior operations leader may be high power / low interest until cutover risk appears.
- Casual users may be low power / low interest until training begins.

The engagement approach should differ for each group.
