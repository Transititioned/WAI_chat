# Solution looking for a problem

## Trigger

A solution has momentum before the problem is clearly defined.

Examples:

- a tool has been selected before requirements are understood
- stakeholders talk about features more than outcomes
- benefits are vague or retrofitted
- the project exists because a product, vendor, or executive idea gained attention
- people ask “how do we implement this?” before asking “what are we solving?”

## What it may mean

The project may be solution-led rather than problem-led.

This creates several risks:

- weak business case
- poor stakeholder buy-in
- overbuilt solution
- missed simpler options
- unrealistic benefits
- resistance from people who do not see the need
- difficulty making scope trade-offs

The solution may still be useful, but the project needs a clearer problem frame.

## What to do next

Reframe without embarrassing the sponsor or vendor.

Ask:

1. What problem are we solving?
2. Who has the problem?
3. How painful or costly is it?
4. What happens if we do nothing?
5. What alternatives exist?
6. Why is this solution the right response?
7. What is the smallest useful version?

Then build a problem-to-solution chain.

## What not to do

Do not publicly imply that the sponsor has backed a bad idea.

Do not become the roadblock who only says no.

Do not proceed straight to delivery planning if the problem definition is still weak.

Do not confuse vendor capability with business need.

## Useful workplace language

“The solution may well be right. I just want to strengthen the problem statement so the delivery case is easier to defend.”

“Before we lock the approach, can we get sharper on the business problem this is solving?”

“If we had to explain why this solution, why now, and why this scope, what would we say?”

## ERP / technology delivery example

A team wants to add an AI chatbot to an internal system because “AI is the direction.”

A problem-led frame might reveal that the actual issue is inconsistent process knowledge and slow answers to common operational questions.

That opens multiple solution paths:

- better SOP search
- process FAQ
- chatbot over approved knowledge
- training refresh
- service desk triage

The AI chatbot may still be the right move, but now it is tied to a real business problem.
