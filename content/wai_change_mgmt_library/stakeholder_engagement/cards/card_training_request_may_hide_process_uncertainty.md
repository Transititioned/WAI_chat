# Training request may hide process uncertainty

## Trigger

Stakeholders repeatedly ask for training, but the underlying process, roles, system behaviour, or support model is still unclear.

You may hear:

- “The business needs more training.”
- “Users will not be ready unless we train them now.”
- “Can we get training material before the process is confirmed?”
- “People are confused; we need a training session.”
- “They keep asking how this will work.”

## What it may mean

The issue may not be training yet.

A training request can hide several different gaps:

- process is not stable
- roles are unclear
- decision rights are unclear
- system behaviour has not been validated
- SOPs are not ready
- support paths are unknown
- exceptions have not been handled
- users are anxious because the change is abstract

Training can only land well when the thing being taught is sufficiently stable.

## What to do next

Separate the need.

Ask:

1. Is this a knowledge gap?
2. Is this a process-design gap?
3. Is this a role clarity gap?
4. Is this a system validation gap?
5. Is this a support-model gap?
6. Is this anxiety because the future state is not yet concrete?

Then choose the right response.

| Underlying gap | Better response |
|---|---|
| Knowledge gap | Training, job aid, demo, FAQ |
| Process gap | Process workshop, SOP validation, decision |
| Role gap | RACI / responsibility clarification |
| System gap | Testing, defect resolution, configuration decision |
| Support gap | Hypercare model, escalation path |
| Anxiety / uncertainty | Knowns/unknowns update and timeline |

## What not to do

Do not use training to cover for unstable process.

Do not train too early on details that are likely to change.

Do not assume attendance equals readiness.

Do not ignore anxiety just because it is expressed as a training request.

## Useful workplace language

“Before we label this as training, can we check whether the process itself is stable enough to teach?”

“That may be a training need, but it may also be a role or process clarity issue.”

“Let’s separate what users need to learn from what the project still needs to decide.”

## Artefact or meeting move

Create a training readiness triage table.

| Request / concern | Knowledge gap? | Process gap? | Role gap? | System gap? | Support gap? | Next action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|  | Yes / No | Yes / No | Yes / No | Yes / No | Yes / No |  |

## ERP / technology delivery example

Users keep asking for training on order entry after an ERP upgrade.

The real issue is that the team has not confirmed which custom fields remain, which status changes matter, and how exceptions will be handled.

The right next step is process/SOP validation before detailed training.
