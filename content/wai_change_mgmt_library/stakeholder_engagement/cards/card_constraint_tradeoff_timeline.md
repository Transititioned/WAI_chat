# Show the trade-off, not a fantasy date

## Trigger

A sponsor, steering group, or senior stakeholder wants a tighter delivery date, but the resources, decisions, environment, vendor support, or business availability needed to make that date credible are not yet committed.

Typical signs:

- The aggressive date assumes named SMEs will be available, but they are not booked.
- The timeline assumes a stable environment, but the environment is not validated.
- The vendor turnaround is critical but not confirmed.
- Business testing or training is compressed without business agreement.
- The project manager is being asked to “just make it work.”

## What it may mean

The organisation is asking for an outcome without accepting the delivery conditions required to make that outcome credible.

This is not just a scheduling problem. It is a governance decision about scope, risk, priority, and support.

## What to do next

Present options instead of arguing for one date.

1. Show the **current-constraint timeline**: what happens if constraints remain as they are.
2. Show the **constraint-resolved timeline**: what becomes possible if specific support is provided.
3. Make the assumptions explicit.
4. Ask governance to choose the option or commit the support.
5. Record the decision and assumptions.

## What not to do

Do not agree to the aggressive date while quietly assuming the missing resources, environment, vendor support, or business capacity will appear later.

Do not let a date become a promise when it is really an unsupported aspiration.

## Useful workplace language

“We can show the earlier date, but only as a supported option. The current-constraint plan lands later.”

“The accelerated plan is possible only if these specific constraints are resolved by these dates.”

“I do not want to pretend the date is just a project-planning choice. It depends on business availability, vendor turnaround, and environment readiness.”

## Artefact or meeting move

### Timeline trade-off table

| Option | Description | Assumptions | Required support | Key risk | Decision needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | Current-constraint timeline | Current resourcing and dependencies remain as-is | None beyond current commitments | Later delivery / longer risk exposure | Endorse realistic baseline |
| B | Accelerated timeline | Named constraints resolved by agreed dates | Sponsor prioritisation, SME time, vendor commitment, environment readiness | Higher execution pressure | Approve support and accept risk |

## ERP / technology delivery example

For an ERP version upgrade, one option may show a later go-live based on current business availability and unresolved customisation validation. A faster option may be possible only if the environment is validated by a specific date, vendor fixes are turned around quickly, and business SMEs are formally committed for testing and process validation.
