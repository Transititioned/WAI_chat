# Silence is not agreement

## Trigger

People are quiet in a meeting, workshop, or governance discussion, and the project assumes they agree.

Later:

- actions do not happen
- decisions are reopened
- people challenge the outcome privately
- business users say they were not consulted
- managers say they “did not realise” the implication
- stakeholders comply on paper but do not support the work

## What it may mean

Silence may mean many things other than agreement.

Possible meanings include:

- people did not understand the implication
- the wrong people were in the room
- people felt unsafe to challenge
- the decision owner was absent
- people needed time to consult others
- the topic was too abstract
- the group was fatigued
- the real objection sits outside the formal meeting

## What to do next

Make agreement active.

1. Ask for confirmation in plain language.
2. Name the implication of the decision.
3. Ask what would stop the action from happening.
4. Give people a safe way to raise concerns.
5. Capture named owners and due dates.
6. Follow up in writing.

Use specific questions instead of “Any questions?”

## What not to do

Do not treat a quiet room as approval.

Do not ask only broad questions such as “Is everyone okay with this?”

Do not move to sign-off if the decision owner is absent or unclear.

Do not punish people for raising late concerns if the original agreement was vague.

## Useful workplace language

“Before we treat this as agreed, can I just check what this means in practice for each area?”

“I am not hearing objections, but I do not want to mistake silence for agreement.”

“What would stop this action from happening after today?”

Use an active agreement check.

## ERP / technology delivery example

In a UAT planning meeting, no one objects to the test schedule. Two weeks later, business SMEs say they cannot attend because peak workload was not considered.

The better move is to ask in the meeting:

“Can each area confirm whether the named SMEs can actually be released for these sessions, and what would stop that happening?”
