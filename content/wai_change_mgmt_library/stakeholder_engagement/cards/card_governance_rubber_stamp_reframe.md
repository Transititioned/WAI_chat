# Governance is for commitments, not theatre

## Trigger

Formal governance exists, but it functions mainly as a status-reporting ritual. Leaders attend, listen, and move on, while unresolved issues, resource conflicts, rumours, and cross-functional blockers continue outside the meeting.

Typical signs:

- The meeting pack is reviewed, but no meaningful commitments are made.
- Risks are reported repeatedly without decisions or owners.
- Executives hear about constraints but do not help remove them.
- Governance approvals are treated as the whole point of the meeting.
- The same issues return month after month.

## What it may mean

The governance forum is underused. It is being treated as a compliance checkpoint rather than a mechanism for aligning leaders, resolving escalated issues, and securing management commitments.

The opportunity is to move from passive reporting to active decision support.

## What to do next

1. Keep the formal reporting concise.
2. Use the available executive audience to focus on decisions, blockers, rumours, conflicts, and commitments.
3. Reconfirm the project purpose where discussion is drifting.
4. Present options for unresolved issues.
5. Ask for specific commitments, not general support.
6. Close the meeting with named actions, owners, and dates.

## What not to do

Do not call a meeting successful just because the pack was accepted.

Do not let governance become theatre where everyone hears about risk but nobody owns the next move.

## Useful workplace language

“The status is in the pack. I’d like to use the time on the two decisions that need leadership input.”

“This issue has been reported for two cycles. Today I’d like to agree the owner and the path to resolution.”

“To keep momentum, the project needs a specific commitment on SME availability rather than general endorsement.”

Meeting move: end every governance meeting with a verbal recap of decisions and commitments, then send the written version within 24 hours.

## ERP / technology delivery example

In an ERP upgrade, do not use the PCG only to report that testing is at risk due to SME availability. Ask for a specific commitment: which leaders will release which people, for how many hours, by what date, and what lower-priority work will be displaced.
