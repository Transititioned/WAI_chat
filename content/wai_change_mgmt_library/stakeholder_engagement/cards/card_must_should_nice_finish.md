# Must / should / nice finish

## Trigger

The project is close to completion, but new requests, tweaks, exceptions, and improvement ideas keep appearing.

You may notice:

- the project is “nearly done” for weeks
- stakeholders want extra reports, screens, fields, or workflow changes
- the team cannot agree what is required for go-live
- every issue is treated as equally important
- the finish line keeps moving

## What it may mean

The project needs explicit finish discipline.

Some items are required for safe go-live. Others are valid improvements but not required for this release. Without classification, stakeholders may experience backlog discipline as dismissal.

## What to do next

Classify remaining work into:

1. **Must** — required for safe go-live, compliance, operational continuity, critical controls, or core business process.
2. **Should** — important but not essential for day-one operation.
3. **Nice** — useful improvement, convenience, or future optimisation.

Then agree the rule:

- Must items block go-live or require explicit risk acceptance.
- Should items need decision and prioritisation.
- Nice items move to backlog unless capacity allows.

## What not to do

Do not let every late request become a go-live blocker.

Do not dismiss valid improvements without capturing them.

Do not use “MVP” as an excuse for unsafe or incomplete delivery.

Do not classify alone. Use business and governance input for contentious items.

## Useful workplace language

“To get this safely across the line, we need to separate must-have for go-live from valid improvements for the next phase.”

“I do not want to lose that request. I also do not want to accidentally make it a blocker if the business can operate without it on day one.”

“Which of these would genuinely stop safe operation, and which can move into the post-go-live backlog?”

## Artefact or meeting move

Create a go-live finish table.

| Item | Category | Why | Go-live impact | Owner | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|  | Must / Should / Nice |  | Blocks / Risk / No block |  | Do now / Defer / Accept risk |

## ERP / technology delivery example

Before ERP go-live, the business requests 50 reports.

The team classifies:

- 5 compliance or operational reports as **Must**
- 12 management reports as **Should**
- 33 convenience or optimisation reports as **Nice**

The project proceeds with the must-have reports and creates a post-go-live reporting backlog.
