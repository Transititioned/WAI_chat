# No-surprises drumbeat

## Trigger

Stakeholders are surprised by bad news, delivery risk, changing dates, unresolved assumptions, or emerging constraints.

You may hear:

- “Why am I only hearing about this now?”
- “This should have been flagged earlier.”
- “The report was green last month.”
- “If this was a risk, why was it not raised?”
- “I need to brief my people before this lands.”

## What it may mean

The project may be treating communication as periodic reporting rather than active expectation management.

The team may be waiting until risk becomes certain before raising it. That can feel safe internally, but it often damages stakeholder trust because leaders lose time to prepare, support, or influence the outcome.

## What to do next

Create a no-surprises communication drumbeat.

1. Flag emerging risks early, even if the impact is not final.
2. Separate confirmed facts from possible impacts.
3. Tell stakeholders what is being done.
4. Say when the next update will come.
5. Escalate privately before public reporting where needed.
6. Keep the message consistent across sponsor, team, governance, and business stakeholders.

## What not to do

Do not hide emerging bad news because the team hopes to fix it first.

Do not overstate uncertain impacts.

Do not let a monthly report be the first time a sponsor hears about a material risk.

Do not communicate every small issue as a crisis. Use judgement and signal materiality.

## Useful workplace language

“Just a heads-up: this has not hit the timeline yet, but it is worth flagging early so no one is caught out.”

“We are still validating the impact. What we know today is this; what we are checking next is that.”

“I wanted to give you an early view before this appears in the formal pack.”

Create a risk communication drumbeat.

## ERP / technology delivery example

The ERP team discovers that a vendor environment issue may affect SIT start dates.

Instead of waiting until the next PCG, the PM gives the sponsor an early heads-up:

“We are not calling a delay yet, but environment validation is now the key watch item. We will know more by Thursday after the vendor check.”

This gives the sponsor time to prepare without creating unnecessary drama.
