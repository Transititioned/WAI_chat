# No surprises before governance

## Trigger

The project team is preparing to present key risks, dates, assumptions, budget figures, resource constraints, or scope trade-offs in a formal governance meeting.

Typical signs:

- The sponsor has not yet seen the difficult message.
- A red or amber item is going into the pack without a prior conversation.
- The project manager is hoping to explain the issue live in the meeting.
- The formal meeting is being used as the first real decision conversation.

## What it may mean

The project is treating governance as a reporting event rather than a stakeholder-management event.

The practical risk is not only that the meeting goes badly. The bigger risk is that the sponsor feels exposed, surprised, or unsupported in front of peers. That damages trust and makes future support harder to secure.

## What to do next

1. Pre-brief the sponsor before the formal meeting.
2. Confirm the message on scope, timeline, cost, resources, assumptions, and constraints.
3. Identify likely objections or pressure points.
4. Agree where the sponsor may need to support, clarify, or make a call.
5. Update the pack so the formal message reflects the agreed framing.
6. Make the decision request clear before the meeting starts.

## What not to do

Do not reveal bad news, difficult trade-offs, unsupported dates, or unresolved assumptions for the first time in the formal governance meeting.

Do not assume that because the issue is written in the pack, the sponsor is ready to defend or explain it.

## Useful workplace language

“I want to walk you through the key messages before the PCG so there are no surprises.”

“There are a couple of constraints where I’d like your guidance on how strongly we position the trade-off.”

“I don’t want this landing cold in the room. Can we spend ten minutes aligning on the message and the ask?”

## ERP / technology delivery example

For an ERP upgrade, do not first reveal in PCG that system testing depends on a validated environment, unresolved customisation fixes, or constrained SME availability. Pre-brief the sponsor, agree the trade-off message, and decide whether the formal ask is a decision, endorsement, escalation, or information item.
