# BAU transition, not project abandonment

## Trigger

The project is approaching the end of hypercare or formal closure, but the business and support teams are not fully confident about ownership.

You may notice:

- users still contact project team members directly
- support queues are unclear
- BAU teams do not know which issues they own
- unresolved defects or enhancements are mixed together
- business owners are unsure what happens next
- the project team wants to close but the business feels exposed

## What it may mean

The transition from project to BAU may be under-defined.

From the project view, go-live may feel complete. From the user view, the change may still be settling. If ownership, support, and backlog handling are unclear, project closure can feel like abandonment.

## What to do next

Create a deliberate BAU transition plan.

Clarify:

1. What support remains during hypercare.
2. When hypercare ends.
3. What BAU support model applies after hypercare.
4. Who owns defects, enhancements, training gaps, process questions, and data issues.
5. What backlog items remain.
6. Which documentation and knowledge must be handed over.
7. What acceptance criteria must be met before project closure.

## What not to do

Do not close the project just because go-live occurred.

Do not leave users to work out support paths themselves.

Do not mix defects, enhancements, training questions, and process gaps in one unmanaged list.

Do not let the project team become permanent informal support because BAU ownership was unclear.

## Useful workplace language

“Go-live is not the same as handover. We need to make sure BAU ownership is clear before the project steps back.”

“I do not want users to feel like the project disappeared after launch. Let’s make the support path explicit.”

“Before we close hypercare, can we separate defects, enhancements, process questions, and training gaps?”

## ERP / technology delivery example

After ERP go-live, users keep sending issues to the project PM.

The project creates a transition matrix:

- true defects go to application support
- process questions go to functional leads
- training gaps go to the training owner
- enhancements go to the post-go-live backlog
- urgent operational issues use the hypercare escalation path until a defined date

This allows the project to close without abandoning the business.
