# Resistance as project memory

## Trigger

Stakeholders react with scepticism before the current project has done much.

You may hear:

- “We tried this before.”
- “It will just get dumped on us.”
- “They will ask for input and ignore it.”
- “The business will be blamed when it does not work.”
- “This is another IT project that does not understand operations.”

## What it may mean

The visible resistance may be project memory.

People may not be rejecting the current change on its merits. They may be responding to previous patterns:

- consultation without influence
- late training
- underfunded business effort
- poor go-live support
- unresolved defects
- project teams leaving after handover
- business SMEs being overloaded
- benefits promised but not delivered

Resistance may be a protective response.

## What to do next

Do not start by defending the project.

1. Ask what has gone wrong before.
2. Listen for repeated themes.
3. Separate valid delivery risks from general cynicism.
4. Convert concerns into actions, assumptions, risks, or decision points.
5. Show where the current approach is different.
6. Be honest where the project still has the same constraints.

## What not to do

Do not label people as resistant too quickly.

Do not say “this time will be different” unless you can explain how.

Do not promise influence where decisions are already fixed.

Do not dismiss historical concerns as negativity.

## Useful workplace language

“That sounds like there may be some history here. Before we talk solution, I’d like to understand what usually goes wrong so we do not repeat it.”

“I am not asking you to be optimistic. I am asking you to help us identify the delivery risks early.”

“If the concern is business effort being underestimated, let’s make that visible rather than leave it as background noise.”

Create a project memory risk log.

## ERP / technology delivery example

Business users are sceptical about an ERP upgrade because previous system changes created extra manual work and poor support after go-live.

The project should not respond with “this is only an upgrade.” It should ask what went wrong last time, then make support, testing, training, and business effort expectations visible in the plan.
